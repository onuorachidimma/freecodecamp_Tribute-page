Prof. Dora Nkem Akunyili

The woman who constantly risked her life so that Nigerians can eat safe.

Here's a summary of Prof. Dora Nkem Akunyili's life:

  • 1954 - Born in Makurdi, Benue State, Nigeria.
  • 1966 - Received her First School Leaving Certificate from St. Patrick's Primary School, Isuofia, Anambra State
  • 1973 - Had her West African School Certificate Examination (W.A.S.C) at Queen of the Rosary Secondary School Nsukka, Enugu State
  • 1973 - Won the Eastern Nigerian Government Post Primary Scholarship and the Federal Government of Nigeria Undergraduate Scholarship
  • 1978 - Studied pharmacology and gradutated at the University of Nigeria (U.N.N.)
  • 1985 - Received her Ph.D. in ethnopharmacology
  • 1981 - Became a Graduate Assistant in the FaU.NN. of Pharmaceutical Sciences, U.N.N
  • 1990 - Became a Senior Lecturer
  • 1996 - Was made a Consultant Pharmacologist at the College of Medicine
  • 1996 - Became Zonal Secretary of the Petroleum Special Trust Fund (P.T.F
  • 2001 - Appointed her the Director-General of the National Agency for Food and Drug Administration and Control (NAFDAC)
  • 2008 - Appointed Minister of Information and Communications
  • 2011 - Ran for election as Senator for Anambra Central but was defeated
  • 2012 - Published her book The War Against Counterfeit Medicine: My Story
  • 2014 - Died of uterine cancer at a specialist hospital in India

Akunyili received over 900 awards throughout her career, the highest number of awards ever received by any Nigerian. Over 100 awards were later discovered in her boxes - Historyville

"Dora Akunyili was a visionary leader who fearlessly fought for truth, transparency, and the well-being of her people. Her dedication to eradicating counterfeit drugs and promoting public health in Nigeria was truly inspiring." - Nelson Mandela
